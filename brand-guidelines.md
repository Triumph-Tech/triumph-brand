# Triumph Tech Brand Guidelines

## Voice & Tone

### Who We Are

Triumph Tech's tone and style are rooted in a balance of innovation, professionalism, and approachability. We communicate with clarity and confidence, ensuring that our expertise shines through in every interaction while remaining relatable and supportive. Our tone is warm and collaborative, fostering a sense of partnership with our clients as we work together to harness technology for growth and engagement. We are solution-focused, tailoring our approach to meet the unique challenges and goals of churches and organizations, and empowering them to leverage digital tools effectively.

Our style reflects simplicity, accessibility, and a modern aesthetic designed to inspire trust and confidence. Every aspect of our communication, from visual design to technical support, is crafted to convey a polished yet approachable identity. We aim to make technology intuitive and engaging, allowing our clients to focus on their missions while we handle the complexities behind the scenes. With a commitment to excellence and a heart for service, Triumph Tech's tone and style reflect our dedication to creating digital experiences that elevate our clients' reach and impact in meaningful ways.

## Logo Usage

### Combination Mark

The combination mark (icon + wordmark) is the primary logo and should be used in most contexts.

### Logo Mark

The icon-only logo mark is used when space is limited or the brand is already established in context.

### Clearspace

Maintain clear space equal to the height of the logo mark on all sides of the logo.

### Approved Backgrounds

The logo may be placed on white, Invictus Blue (#0A2540), or the first Invictus Blue tone (#0E3258). Avoid busy backgrounds or unapproved colors.

## Image Wash

When using photography as a subtle background in graphic applications, apply the following layer treatment in your design software:

1. Desaturate the image to 0% saturation
2. Set image opacity to 15% with Luminosity blending mode
3. Place Invictus Blue (#0A2540) as a solid color layer beneath

## Embellishments

Embellishments are blueprint-style decorative elements used to add visual texture and brand character to layouts. They are rendered in gray and are suitable for both light and dark contexts. Each embellishment has a defined role — use them intentionally and avoid mixing multiple types in the same composition.

**Web** — Place behind hero sections or full-width content blocks to add depth and visual texture. Intended as a large-scale background fill. Keep it behind all foreground elements and reduce opacity as needed.

**Arc** — Use at section edges or corners to frame content and introduce organic curvature to an otherwise structured layout. Works well as a transitional element between sections.

**Registration** — Apply in compositions where technical precision is the emphasis — callouts, data-driven sections, or layouts that benefit from an engineered aesthetic. Use sparingly to preserve its intentional feel.

**Gear** — Deploy in technical or product-focused contexts to reinforce themes of process, automation, and innovation. Use sparingly as a supporting detail rather than a dominant element.

All embellishment assets are available as SVG and PNG (256, 512, 1024 px) in `assets/embellishments/`. Refer to `brand_elements.embellishments` in `brand-kit.json` for the full structured reference.
